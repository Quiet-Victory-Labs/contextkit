## BYOMCP vs Hosted MCP

<Card title="What is BYOMCP?" icon="information">
  **BYOMCP** (Bring Your Own MCP) means you run the MCP server yourself — locally via `context serve --stdio` or self-hosted via `context serve --http`. Your semantic plane stays on your machine or your infrastructure. This is the default for the Free tier and works perfectly for solo developers.

  **Hosted MCP** means ContextKit runs the MCP server for you at `mcp.runcontext.dev`. Your AI tools connect to a managed endpoint with authentication, so your whole team shares the same semantic plane without anyone running a local server. Available on Team and Enterprise plans.
</Card>

## FAQ

### Is the CLI really free?

Yes. The ContextKit CLI is open-source (MIT license). You can introspect databases, build your semantic plane, lint, enrich, and serve via local MCP — all for free, forever. No API keys, no cloud account, no time limits.

### What counts as a "connector"?

A connector is a managed database connection hosted by ContextKit. On the Team plan, you get 3 connectors — for example, one PostgreSQL, one Snowflake, and one BigQuery. The Enterprise plan includes unlimited connectors. On the Free plan, you connect to databases directly from your machine (BYOMCP).

### Can I try Team features for free?

Contact us for a 14-day trial of the Team plan. No credit card required.

### What happens if I exceed my seat limit?

Additional seats on the Team plan are $10/seat/month. Enterprise plans include unlimited seats.

### Do you offer annual billing?

Yes. Annual billing saves 20% on Team and Enterprise plans. Contact us for details.
